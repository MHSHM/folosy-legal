Delete your Folosy data

Last updated: July 23, 2026

This page explains how to delete data associated with the Folosy mobile application for Android (package name com.folosy.app), developed and published by Folosy.

Folosy is a local-first app: your financial data lives on your own phone and, if you enable backup, in your own Google Drive. We do not operate any server that stores your personal or financial data, so most deletion is directly in your hands and takes effect immediately. Full details are in our privacy policy.

1. Delete the data stored on your phone

Your transactions, balance, budgets, categories, settings, and processed bank SMS messages are stored only in the app’s private storage on your device. To delete them:

  • Individual entries: delete any transaction or category inside the app.
  • Everything at once: open Android Settings → Apps → Folosy → Storage → Clear storage, or simply uninstall the app.

Either action removes all local data immediately and permanently.

2. Delete your Google Drive backups

If you enabled backup, copies of the app’s database are stored in a hidden, app-specific folder in your own Google Drive. To delete them:

  • Open Google Drive → Settings → Manage apps → find Folosy → choose Delete hidden app data (and/or disconnect the app). Google then deletes the folder and every backup in it.
  • You can also turn off backup inside the app at any time to stop new backups from being created.

Backups also delete themselves automatically: the app keeps at most one backup per day for the last 14 days and removes older files.

3. Request deletion of subscription data

If you purchased a premium subscription, our subscription-management provider (RevenueCat) holds a minimal purchase record: the purchase token issued by Google Play, a randomly generated app user identifier (not your name or email), and basic device information. To have this record deleted:

  • Email folosy4@gmail.com with the subject line “Data deletion request”.
  • We will process the deletion and confirm to you by reply within 30 days.

Google Play keeps its own record of your purchases under Google’s policies; you can manage or cancel subscriptions in the Google Play subscriptions page.
